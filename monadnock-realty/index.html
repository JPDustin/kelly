The Approach

The gravel crunches beneath your feet as you walk up the circular drive, flanked by century-old sugar maples that Samuel Whitmore planted himself. 1847: A Vision Takes Shape

1847

The Textile King's Dream

Samuel Whitmore gazed across the rolling meadows and envisioned more than just a home— he saw a legacy. Fresh from his success in Peterborough's booming textile industry, Whitmore commissioned the finest architects and craftsmen from Boston to create a mansion worthy of New England's industrial aristocracy.

1860

A Station of Freedom

When the nation tore itself apart over slavery, the Whitmore family quietly joined the Underground Railroad. Behind the library's mahogany bookshelf lies a hidden passage that sheltered freedom seekers on their journey north. The estate's remote location and Whitmore's influence made it the perfect sanctuary.

1920

Modern Conveniences, Timeless Character

The Jazz Age brought electricity and indoor plumbing to the estate. The kitchen was lovingly updated with period-appropriate fixtures that honored the home's heritage while embracing the conveniences of the modern world—a philosophy that continues to guide its stewardship today.

2025

Your Chapter Awaits

Today, the Whitmore Estate stands ready for its next guardian. Three families have cherished this home across nearly two centuries. Each added their own chapter while preserving the soul of this remarkable place. What story will you write within these storied walls?

The Living Landscape

Twelve Acres of New England Poetry

Beyond the formal gardens lies a landscape that captures the essence of New England's natural beauty. Stone walls built by Samuel Whitmore's own hands still define property boundaries, while the sugar maple grove he planted provides breathtaking autumn color that rivals any Vermont postcard.

The meadow stretches toward distant hills, offering space for contemplation or celebration. Here, three generations have hosted garden parties, children have built snow forts, and couples have exchanged vows beneath the ancient oak that predates even the house itself.

A meandering stone path leads to a private pond where Samuel once skated with his children in winter and cooled his feet during summer planning sessions. It's a place where time moves differently, where the pressures of modern life dissolve into the gentle rhythm of seasons and years.
